About Me

I am a dedicated researcher and software engineer with over 3.5 years of experience, specializing in edge computing, machine learning, and traffic fingerprinting systems. At the University of Nevada, Reno, I’ve worked extensively on the AlertWildFire system, integrating edge devices like Nvidia Jetson to optimize wildfire detection, reducing detection time by 70% and network bandwidth usage by 51%. My research focuses on developing lightweight machine learning frameworks that balance accuracy with computational overhead, achieving significant improvements in performance for resource-constrained environments. I’ve collaborated on multiple research papers accepted at top-tier conferences, including IEEE INFOCOM and GLOBECOM. One of my key projects, "FlexiNet," demonstrates a 7% reduction in processing time and a 56% decrease in memory usage while maintaining high accuracy. Previously, as a Software Engineer at REVE Systems, I engineered solutions for customer engagement platforms, enhancing performance through asynchronous systems and reengineering the onboarding process with React.js and Spring Boot. I am passionate about problem-solving, focusing on algorithms, system design, and dynamic programming. In my free time, I contribute to open-source projects and regularly participate in competitive programming challenges. Feel free to explore my projects and publications to learn more about my work.
